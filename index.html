Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our hot air balloon safaris in Uganda.

What is the best time of year for a hot air balloon safari in Uganda?

The best time for hot air balloon safaris in Uganda is during the dry seasons from December to February and June to September. During these periods, weather conditions are most favorable with clearer skies and better wildlife visibility.

How long does the entire experience last?

The complete experience typically lasts 3-4 hours. This includes pre-flight preparation and briefing (30-45 minutes), the actual flight (approximately 1 hour), and the post-flight champagne breakfast (about 1 hour).

What should I wear for a hot air balloon safari?

We recommend wearing comfortable, layered clothing as temperatures can vary. Closed-toe shoes are essential, and don't forget your camera! Early mornings can be cool, but it warms up as the sun rises.

Is there an age or health restriction for participants?

Children must be at least 7 years old and accompanied by an adult. Participants should be able to stand for the duration of the flight (approximately 1 hour) and have reasonable mobility. Those with serious health conditions or who are pregnant should consult with their doctor before booking.

What happens if weather conditions are not suitable for flying?

Safety is our top priority. If weather conditions are unsuitable, we will reschedule your flight for the next available date or provide a full refund. The final decision to fly is made by our pilot on the morning of your scheduled flight.
